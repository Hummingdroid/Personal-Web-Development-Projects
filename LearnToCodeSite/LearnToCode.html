....Become a programmer

When I first started to learn to code...scratch that, When I first looked into coding I thought why not.
I mean, I loved how the technology that was portrayed in Science Fiction films was seemingly always coming
out of the wood work everyday it seemed. Cellphones went from the size of Websters Dictonaries into things
so small that at times seemed rather inconvientent. That was in the early 2000's. Then came 2007, the year
of "Android". Oh Android, that beautiful open source goodness. In case you the reader whom ever you may be
dont already know, open source is this thing, that basically means...anyone can work on it..free code boys
and girls! YAY! So after the birth of Android, I decided to work on testing and buying mobile devices up
the wazoo. From mobile PDA to things that only lasted a few months, each being consumed by me and sold for
a little loss just so I could get my hands on the newest technology. Then came the day in 2012 that I bucked
up and finally went back to school to persue my CSIS Degree and learn to code.

Well as you probably have noticed if you have ever looked at code for the first time it looked like egyptian
sandscrit when I first laid eyes on it. Then came the professors and youtube videos and books galore to help
me decypher this alien langugage. Some beleive it is actually from aliens and if you work in the field long
enough I'm sure you'll meet some Software Engineers that may possibly be from Mars. Yeah Software ninjas are
a unique bunch but one thing you will notice is... they truly are...most of them the coolest, smartest, funnest
people this side of universe!

So what does this all mean and why do you care? Because you want to learn to code. Because when you wake up in
morning you "KNOW" that you are meant for great things and that the future is yours to take a part of. Because
YOU are the future and you are amazing. Best part of programming is that you can be in every field and don't
have to stake your life on one field forever and turn into a mindless drone. You are, by learning to code,
becoming a critical part of life as we know it and thats not all. Programming is in computers and computers are
EVERYWHERE! Snowboarding? computers make those now, Fast cars? CPU's make you fast and furious, Humanitarian work?
mobile phone apps are now being used to do eye exams on people in third world countries to help kids and adults
alike see for the first time and have a chance at reading and seeing the world as an amazing things you are making
better everyday! Pretty awesome right? Well if you've read this far and want me to point you to your future check
out the links below.

Something to remember while you learn.

  1. If you get stuck on a problem, take a mental break and think of something else entirely. Let your brain do the work.
  2. Never be afraid to ask for help. If you don't know about something, reach out on a forum. There are always people eager to help and share their knowledge.
  3. Always look back on what you have accomplished whenever you get discouraged. Tough it out and the reward will always come.

And as always...

  • "K.I.S.S" Keep it super simple
  • It's not the destination thats the important part, but the journey and how you got there.
